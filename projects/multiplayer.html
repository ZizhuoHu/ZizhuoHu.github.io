Broken Bridge

A symmetrical Quake III capture-the-flag arena built around a fractured central crossing, layered routes, vertical control, and risk-versus-reward movement.

Project Info

  • Quake III Arena
  • Tool: GtkRadiant
  • Role: Level Designer
  • Mode: Capture the Flag

Overview

Broken Bridge is a 10-player Quake III Arena CTF map set in the ruins of a human city. Mirrored red and blue bases frame a fractured midfield where elevated platforms, lower pits, jump pads, and lava hazards create distinct attack and recovery routes. The original design target was a 30-minute match in which the first team to capture eight flags wins.

Design Development

Iteration & Design Intent

The map evolved from a paper plan into a playable symmetrical arena through repeated comparison between route readability, combat value, and capture-the-flag timing. The goal was not simply to mirror geometry, but to create equivalent strategic opportunities for both teams.

01
Core Concept

A Ruined Gothic Crossing

The central broken bridge became the visual and mechanical identity of the map. Cathedral-like towers, tall structural silhouettes, and a blood-red sky support the post-apocalyptic atmosphere, while the bridge gives players an immediate landmark for understanding which side of the arena they occupy.

02
Competitive Foundation

Symmetry with Route Choice

Red and blue territories use mirrored geometry so neither team receives an inherent spatial advantage. Within that symmetry, upper ramps, lower pits, jump pads, and side approaches create choices between speed, safety, visibility, and resource access. The direct route is legible but exposed; longer paths offer cover and recovery opportunities.

03
LDD Revision

Clarifying the Midfield

The first plan used broad yellow and orange zones to separate teams and hazardous space. In the updated LDD, I unified the primary walkable surfaces and concentrated lava into distinct pockets. This made the playable boundary clearer, strengthened the bridge as a focal point, and created more deliberate moments where players must choose between a safe rotation and a dangerous shortcut.

04
Combat Economy

Weapons, Resources, and Control

Railguns support the long cross-map sightlines, while rockets and shotguns reinforce close-range fights around ramps, stairs, and flag-room entrances. Yellow armor, health, ammunition, and the central power-up pull players away from passive defense and turn specific routes into temporary control objectives.

05
Playable Translation

From Diagram to Arena

During implementation, I preserved the LDD's red-blue readability and overall route hierarchy while testing actual travel time, jump-pad landings, exposure, and item access. Future playtests would continue tuning spawn safety, flag-run timing, weapon dominance, and resource respawn values so visual symmetry also produces fair competitive flow.
