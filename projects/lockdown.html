Lockdown

A Half-Life 2 prison level built around layered combat spaces, shifting routes, environmental storytelling, and an escalating break from containment.

Project Info

  • Half-Life 2 · Source Engine
  • Role: Level Designer
  • Single-Player FPS
  • Tool: Hammer Editor

Overview

Lockdown is a single-player prison level that moves the player through cell blocks, damaged service spaces, elevated guard routes, and an exterior escape. I used the familiar language of gates, fences, security rooms, and broken walls to make progression readable while maintaining the pressure of a hostile detention facility.

Combat PacingPlayer FlowVerticalityEnvironmental Storytelling

Design Goals

The level begins with compressed routes and controlled sightlines, then gradually opens into larger, multi-level encounters. Bars and security fences let the player preview threats before entering a space, while breached walls, vents, and damaged architecture create clear transitions and alternate routes.

Encounter & Flow

Combat spaces combine ground-level cover with upper walkways and guarded positions, encouraging the player to read threats across multiple elevations. Quieter rooms provide short pacing breaks and environmental story beats before the level builds toward its exterior helicopter encounter.

Development Process

From LDD to RTM

I developed Lockdown through milestone-based iteration. Each stage was tested as a playable experience, then revised around player guidance, combat pressure, spatial readability, and pacing before the next layer of production was added.

01
Planning

Level Design Document

The LDD established the complete route across two prison floors, the exterior yard, and the final escape. I mapped locked and unlocked doors, stairs, force fields, combat beats, helicopter pressure, resource placement, and key wow moments so the level had a clear escalation before production began.

02
Spatial Validation

Whitebox

The first playable blockout focused on scale and circulation. I validated cell-block navigation, vertical connections, the relationship between interior spaces and the yard, and whether the critical path stayed understandable without relying on final art.

03
First Playable

Initial Gameplay

Doors, force fields, triggers, and early enemy encounters turned the layout into a full playable route. Playtests exposed unclear sightlines and uneven pressure, so I adjusted cover, encounter spacing, navigation cues, and transitions between quiet and high-intensity areas.

04
Systems Complete

Gameplay Complete

At gameplay complete, the full prison route, encounter sequence, exterior yard, and helicopter finale were functional. I tuned enemy pressure, resource distribution, recovery spaces, and combat duration so the difficulty curve built consistently toward the escape.

05
Visual Pass

Aesthetics

The aesthetic pass introduced prison architecture, materials, props, fencing, lighting, and environmental storytelling. Every visual layer was evaluated against gameplay readability so important routes, threats, and interactive elements remained easy to identify.

06
Final Validation

RTM / Ready to Launch

The RTM pass focused on reliability and presentation: collision cleanup, scripting fixes, performance checks, final lighting, and repeated end-to-end playthroughs. I removed visual noise and resolved remaining friction so the finished level delivered the intended flow from the first cell block to the final escape.
